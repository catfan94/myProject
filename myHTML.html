My life

Hi, everybody. My name is Mackenzie and I'm here to tell you about why I'm interested in becoming a web developer. This is my first project, so consider this a blog if you will. Where should I start? Well, I guess I can indulge into some things about me. I'm a Floridian, born and raised; and no, I never heard of or even met this mysterious "Florida man". It sounds like something made up by anti-floridians, if even a term exists. There's not much to say about my social life, considering I'm more introverted. Of course, I long to be more social, but with the world being obsessed with politics and other things that bore me, it's hard to engage with individuals that live, breathe, and sleep politics. I suppose this stems from my father always talking/posting about it. Being introverted also means I don't express myself in the usual way. It's easy for people to talk about their feelings, but with me it's different. I don't know why, I just never thought people cared enough. I don't want to this to become a diary of sorts, so I'll get to it then. I never knew what I wanted to be when I was younger. Even when I reached into my older teens, I didn't know what interested me the most. At first, I always liked the idea with working with my hands. Something artistic like glass blowing or weilding. Of course, being female, I never considered the physical limitations that the career fields I was interested in had. Nevertheless, there was one career that I invested my time in; that being a dental assistant. At first, I've enjoyed my time in the dental field, but as time progressed, it seemed it just wasn't for me. So after 4 years of dental assisting, I've finally decided to change my career. I had to think long and hard about what actually interested me the most, as this would be my third career change I've done (yes, I said THIRD career change. My first choice was physical therapist assistant). After a while, I decided to go into the tech world. Now I've been in online courses for over a year and I have about one more to go for my Associate's. I'm really enjoying these courses, although some were more difficult than others. When I chose web development as my major, I originally chose to do front-end, where I'd be working mostly on how the webpage looked like. But as I progressed with my courses, I came to realize that I enjoy programming as well. I've been wathcing some video tutorials before and actually currently in my courses. So now I feel compelled to be a full-stack developer. This sounds more exicting to do than just work on physical appearances of a website.


My journey

During the course of 2 1/2 years, I've done some practice HTML sites and kept my prototypes for references. In other words, I kept these files so when I finally decided to make a real project, I would have all the codes I needed to make a real, functioning website. While I can't wait to create a project, I know there's still a lot to learn. So for now, this will be like my first project. If this blog is posted on the internet, then I'll most likely add an comments section on here. It'll be interesting to see what others write on here. I believe that covers most of what I intended to write on here.
